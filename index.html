Air from the lungs passes through the vocal cords (Swarayantra) at speed. The muscles in the larynx stretch or relax, and the waves produced in this process are called sound/voice.

The sound vibrations of word-pronunciations resonate within the human body. They open up the ultra-fine pores of internal organs — including lifeless, stunned, numbed, and contracted organs — creating healing through vibration alone.
Pronunciation → Physical Reaction
Different words cause different reactions: belching, yawning, flatulence, sneezing, perspiration, lip-vibration. These are not accidents — they are evidence of internal organs being exercised through pronunciation.
Bloodstream Delivery
Vibrations created by the vocal apparatus enter the bloodstream and travel through the complete circulatory system — reaching every organ through the intricate network of arteries and veins.

Case Study — The Word 'Paani' (Water)
PAANI
P
Lips press
AA
Mouth opens
NI
Sky direction
A mute, thirsty human in a forest needed to communicate the need for water. Their dry lips pressed and opened — creating 'Pa'. Pointing upward to the sky where rain comes from — adding 'Ni'. The word 'Paani' was born from physical need, body movement, and environmental gesture.
Why 'Ni' and not 'Na' or 'Nee'?
The exact vowel sound 'i' in 'Ni' was determined by the specific mouth position of a thirsty person pointing upward. Each variation in mouth shape corresponds to a different physical state — and a different sound. This is not arbitrary. It is anatomical.
Why Country Names Matter
Bharat — the 'Bh' Voice
The 'Bh' sound in Bharat is a labial aspirate — produced by pressing lips and releasing air with force. This specific vibration creates a resonance that affects the chest, heart, and lungs. It is why the land of Bharat carries a specific bodily significance.
India — the 'In' Sound
The 'In' phoneme is a nasal-palate combination activating the upper respiratory tract and brain sinuses. Every pronunciation of the country's name subtly exercises these pathways.
Hindustan — the 'H' Aspiration
Beginning with a full aspiration 'H' — a deep glottal breath — followed by 'indu' (water/moon) and 'stan' (place). Each segment activates a different organ system through its pronunciation sequence.

Healing Through Word Vibration
NowssB is your daily word practice app. Every word is a frequency. Every frequency has a body target. Correct pronunciation = correct healing.
How It Works
1
Sign up — answer 4 personalization questions about your goal, timing, level and gender
↓
2
Get your words — a personal word prescription built around your body goals
↓
3
Open the app — morning, evening, or night — your routine is already ready
↓

Practice words — in the Walkman-style player with AI pronunciation scoring
↓
5
Earn mastery — score 90+ three times in a row to master a word permanently
Today's Practice
Time-aware routine
The home card automatically detects your current time and loads the matching routine — Morning before 10am, Midday 10am–1pm, Afternoon 1–5pm, Evening 5–9pm, Night after 9pm. Tap it to start immediately.
The Word Player
Walkman-style experience
A spinning disc, waveform bars, and 5 mode tabs — all on one screen. No scrolling. Everything is one tap away.
Listen
Plays the word audio. Phonetic syllable chips light up as each sound plays. Male or Female voice — toggle top right.
Record
Tap the red button → speak the word → AI scores your pronunciation 0–100. Play back your recording. Redo anytime.
Repeat
Count your repetitions — 3, 7, or 21 times. Each tap registers one rep. Progress bar fills to completion.
Meaning
Shows the organ this word targets, the healing benefit, the full meaning, and the natural language origin.
Guide
Correct mouth position, resonance point in the body, the most common mistake, and your practice tip.
→
After all words finish — a healing sentence combining every word you just practiced plays automatically. This closes every session.
My Routines
5 personal routine slots
Morning · Midday · Afternoon · Evening · Night. Rename any slot and set any time. The NOW badge shows which routine matches right now.
Words tab — your practice list for this routine
Library tab — all words you own, tap + to add to this routine
History tab — past sessions with word count and completion
Start Session — launches the player with all routine words
Pencil icon — rename the routine or change its time slot

Coach Tips
1
Always use the Guide tab first for a new word — correct mouth position makes the difference between feeling nothing and feeling the resonance.
2
The Record tab is your real coach. Aim for 70+ to start, 90+ to master. Don't skip it.
3
Do 21 repetitions of any word in the Repeat tab for maximum cellular resonance effect. 7 minimum per session.
4
Practice at the same time daily — your routine detects the time automatically. Consistency builds the frequency pattern in the body.
5
The healing sentence at session end is not optional — it binds all the words you practiced into one unified frequency. Always let it play.
